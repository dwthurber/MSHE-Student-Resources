Definition

The issues, controversies, or concerns that guide the need for conducting astudy and stands on its own

Purpose

Define the paramaters of the research study and direct the focus of the rest of the research project.

Process

To Locate the Research Problem, Ask:
  • What was the issue, problem, or controversy that you want to address?
  • What controversy leads to a need for this study?
  • What is the concern being addressed “behind” this study?

Research Question Development

Different Parts of Research: Example of Research Topic, Research Problem, Purpose, and Research Question

For example:

  • Topic – Distance learning in higher education programs
  • Problem –Lack of students in distance education courses in higher education administration
  • Purpose – To study why students don’t enroll in theseclasses and see how the curriculum might change
  • Research Question –How does the use of technologyin the classroom change the curriculum and/or deterstudents from enrolling in a distance education class at a Research I institution?

Should the Problem Be Researched?

Study the Research Problem, If You Can:

  • Gain Access to People and Sites?
  • Find Time, Locate Resources, and Use your Skills
  • Justify the Importance of the Problem: others report in literature, experiences others have had, your personal experiences
Study the Research Problem, If the study:
  • Will fill a gap or void of existing literature?
  • Advance knowledge? Importance?
  • Replicates a past study but examines different participants and different research sites
  • Extends past research or examines the topic more thoroughly
  • Gives voice to people silenced, not heard, or rejected in society
  • Importance, informs/contributes to practice

Questions: Guidelines

  • Expect the questions to change as your understanding grows
  • Ask neutral questions, no direction needed – no bias
  •  Begin with how or what rather than why (associated with quantitative research more)
  • Use words such as generate, discover, understand, describe, explore
  • Try to avoid words such as affect, relate, compare, determine, cause, and influence – suggests cause-and effect
  • Write the central question and sub-questions (cluster)
  •  Identify the participants in the study• Mention the research site for the study if possible
