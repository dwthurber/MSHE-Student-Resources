Definition

The abstract should be:

  • 120 words or less
  • 1-2 sentences to introduce the research topic and question
  • 1 sentence to explain the data collection methods
  • 1-2 sentences to explain the findings and importance of the study

Process

Qualities of a Good Abstract:

  • Is concise – no more than 120 words in length
  • Includes the main components of your project:
    • Purpose, research question (or summarized research question), importance of the study, methods, results and key findings, conclusion
  • Follows the same order/chronology of your project
  • Provides logical connections between the information presented
  • Adds no new information – just summarizes
  • Contains key information to entice the reader to read the rest of the paper

Avoid:

  • Copying key sentences from your MP: this tends to result in too much or too little information
  • Starting with “this paper” or “this report” – just write about the research
  • Explaining the sections of the paper (e.g. “The data collection section describes the different sources and processes I used to collect information on my topic.”)
  • Starting sentences with “it is suggested that…” or “it is believed that…”
  • Abbreviations and acronyms, if possible
