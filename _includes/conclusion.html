Definition

The conclusion is a “space to argue for the importance of the study” and pull the research ‘story’ together

Process

Conclusion Suggestions

  1. Conclude by returning to the introduction
    • Return to the initial ideas, concepts, examples, facts or themes presented
    • Be sure not to introduce new concepts
    • Tie the parts together to bring closure
  2. Summarize key findings
    • Don’t’ repeat key findings verbatim
    • Reiterate and reinforce the ‘take away’ points
  3. Acknowledge the lack of conclusion to the study itself
    • It is important to acknowledge when there is no definite conclusion to be made
    • Often a study leaves more questions to be asked
  4. Ensure that the ‘so what’ questions are answered
    • Show the implications and illustrate the consequences
    • Present the importance and implication(s) clearly
  5. Speculate about the future of the topic
    • As the expert or authority on the topic, you should discuss what future research lies ahead
  6. Consider the methodological implications of the study (Discussion)
    • Discuss methodological issues that arose
    • Provide recommendations to future researchers

Conclusion MP Checklist

  • Discusses implications/significance of the research findings for the field.
  • Summarizes the main findings of the study, including the interventions, recommendations, or new models suggested by your research.
  • Discusses suggestions for next steps in future research.
  • Discusses new questions that emerged as part of the MP.
