Definition

The Abstract should be:

  • 120 words or less
  • 1-2 sentences to introduce
  • 1 sentence to explain the methods
  • 1-2 sentences to explain findings or importance

Qualities of a Good Abstract:

  • Is concise – no more than 120 words in length
  • Includes the main areas of your master’s project:
  • Purpose, RQ (or summarized RQ), importance of the study, methods, results and key findings, conclusion
  • Follows the same order/chronology of your master’s project
  • Provides logical connections between the information presented
  • Adds no new information – just summarizes
  • Contains key information to entice the reader to read the rest of your paper.

Try to Avoid:

  • Copying key sentences from your MP: too much or too little information.
  • Starting with “This paper” or “This report” – just write about the research
  • Explaining sections of the paper
  • Starting sentences with “It is suggested that . .” or “It is believed that . .”
  • Abbreviations, acronyms if possible
