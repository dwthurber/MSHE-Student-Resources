A Description & Chronology


The Master’s Project is an opportunity to pursue resolution of a question about which you have genuine concern. The question may grow out of an interest that you have had for some time or one that has arisen recently through course work or personal experience in a work setting or internship. As you identify and pursue resolution of your question, you will learn a great deal about your topic and about yourself. During this process you will acquire tools for inquiry, assessment, and self-reflection that will be useful throughout your career. The Master’s Project is a major undertaking and is usually completed across the span of a year. It involves three courses: MSHE 406: Research and Analysis I, MSHE 407: Research and Analysis II, and MSHE 408: Research and Analysis III.


Fall Quarter

MSHE 406: Research and Analysis I: Question Development, Literature Review, Data Collection Planning

  • Develop and refine a research question
  • Form peer research support group(s) – instructors, coaches, peers, and other experts
  • Read in the topic area of your question
  • Acquire research knowledge (qualitative, quantitative, and mixed methods) and methods/skills (interview, using existing surveys and artifact analysis, observation) through researchers, practitioners, and research examples
  • Complete a rationale for MP
  • Define the domains of your question and reduce/synthesize the existing literature on your research question
  • Present preliminary literature outline and ideas to collect data
  • Complete introduction and background of MP and literature review
  • Complete preliminary plan for collecting data and submitting to IRB

Winter Quarter

MSHE 407: Research and Analysis II: Research Methodology and Data Collection

  • Continue collaboration with instructors, coaches, peers, and experts for feedback
  • Continue to learn data collection methods and techniques – surveys, interviews, artifact analysis, observation – including constructing instruments/protocols used and how to analyze the data, using classroom examples
  • Complete plan for ethical, field-based and/or existing data collection; submit for approval to Institutional Review Board, including appropriate recruitment and consent forms
  • Learn about coding and analyzing data
  • Collect and start to analyze data
  • Present at a poster session on research in class
  • Revise MPQ, introduction/background, and literature review

Spring Quarter

MSHE 408: Research and Analysis III: Analysis, Interpretation, and Dissemination

  • Complete data collection and coding by start of course
  • Continue collaboration with instructors, coaches, peers, and other experts
  • Analyze and summarize data – learn to display and reduce data
  • Interpret findings and integrate them with the literature review
  • Write conclusions
  • Present research to an audience
  • Prepare/organized finished copy of MP
  • Complete executive summary
