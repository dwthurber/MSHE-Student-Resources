Definition

Identifies the research problem, sets the context, states the question, and identifies the sections of the MP that will follow

Purpose

Introduce the research topic and question, as well as each section of the project, to give readers an idea of the focus and layout of the paper

Process

Definitions and Concepts

  • Define specialized terms and jargon for readers unfamiliar with the topic.
  • Briefly outline central concepts of the topic.

Statement of the Problem/Research Issue

  • Describe the problem or phenomenon that inspired the research project.
  • Describe the central concept of the research question and the question itself.

Importance of Project

  • Explain how this project can contribute to the field of study.

Overview of MP Sections—briefly describe the following:

  • Literature Review Domains
  • Data Collection Methods
  • Data Summary
  • Implications for Research and Practice

General Tips

  • When writing the introduction and background, assume the reader knows nothing about the research topic. Define all specialized terms and concepts.
  • Think of the introduction and background as a “road map” for your overall paper—it should show readers what you will be writing about and the order in which everything appears.
  • Keep the introduction and background relatively concise—it usually should not go over 2-3 pages.
  • Return to your introduction and background regularly as you write your paper. As sections, concepts, and conclusions change, those changes will need to be reflected in your introduction.
